About

Comparative Politics
with a Regional Focus

I am a PhD student in Political Science (Comparative Politics) at the University of Alabama, where I also serve as a Graduate Teaching Assistant. My research sits at the intersection of political sociology and comparative politics.

Before joining Alabama, I was a Lecturer of Sociology at Gono University in Dhaka, where I taught Urban Sociology, Gender Studies, Social Inequality, and Political Sociology. I hold a Master's degree in Sociology from the University of Dhaka.

My fieldwork and research projects have taken me across Bangladesh — studying Munda communities in the Sundarbans, climate-induced displacement, school dropout in urban slums, and the July 2024 protest cycle. I am currently a Research Assistant to Dr. Holger Albrecht, working on Gen-Z protest movements.
